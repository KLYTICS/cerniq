### Cryptographic posture

- **Library:** `@noble/ed25519` v2.1.0 — identical to `apps/api/src/common/crypto/ed25519.util.ts`
- **Curve:** Ed25519 (RFC 8032)
- **Signing:** `ed.signAsync` over canonical-JSON-encoded UTF-8 payloads
- **Verification:** `ed.verifyAsync` against the signing public key
- **Canonical form:** keys sorted recursively, `undefined` values dropped (matches `JSON.stringify` semantics + AEGIS audit-chain canonical form)

### What this proves

- The verify path's documented denial precedence (CLAUDE.md §6) is consistent end-to-end.
- RFC 9396 RAR evaluator (peer bf9d6030's promotion) handles per-order, per-day, and trading-hours constraints correctly under cumulative-day workloads.
- MCP per-tool action scoping (peer 2b178d04's H-2 hardening) prevents `tools/call` escalation in a real Ed25519-signed token flow.

- Multi-tenant isolation by `principalId` (invariant #5) holds against valid-signature cross-tenant probes.
- The audit chain's hash-link + per-row Ed25519 signature detect tampering at the exact row that was mutated.
- Intent manifest reconciliation (ADR-0017) flags amount drift above 1% tolerance and accepts drift below.
